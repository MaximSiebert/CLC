Educate, Equip, Empower.

Our mission is to equip and empower all Canadians to thrive in the digital economy.

We envision a prosperous Canada in which all people have the knowledge and confidence to harness the power of technology to achieve economic and personal fulfillment.

View our educational programs

The new Canadian landscapes are digital.

At Canada Learning Code, we believe that technical skills are tools of empowerment. Our goal is to ensure that all Canadians – particularly women, girls, people with disabilities, Indigenous youth and newcomers – who have been historically underrepresented in the sector are given equal opportunity to build our future.

Our Vision

We are champions for coding education in Canada, driving results through program design and delivery, strategic industry and public partnerships, educator training, research, advocacy and awareness.

We aim to empower Canadians in every province and territory to develop computational thinking abilities, through code and other digital skills, equipping them to become creators – not just consumers – of technology.

We believe that diversity is Canada’s strength and everyone should be given equal opportunity to learn critical 21st century skills. Our goal is to level the educational playing field to drive innovation.

Educational

Programs

Our educational programs are designed for specific audiences to maximize our impact and support engaged communities.

Offers beginner-friendly workshops for adults who want to learn computer programming and other technical skills in a social, collaborative way.

Our adult programming offers hands-on, project-based learning experiences that are designed to give beginners the skills and confidence they need to become digital creators.

Our workshops and educational events are open to adults of all ages. Workshops cover everything from introductory HTML & CSS, to WordPress, Python, Ruby, web design and more. They are especially popular and successful because of the 4:1 ratio of students to volunteer mentors at every workshop, which is only possible because of overwhelming support from the technology and startup communities in the cities in which we operate.

Designed specifically for girls ages 6 to 13 to help them see technology as a medium for self-expression, and a means for making an impact on the world.

Girls Learning Code programs encourage girls to foster an interest in technology and understand there is a place for them in the tech landscape. We run week-long camps, after school programs, field trips, workshops and “We’ll Take Your Kids to Work” days where we bring girls on a tour of local technology companies to learn more about careers in tech. Programs cover a variety of topics from programming languages like HTML & CSS and Ruby, to image editing and blog creation, to 3D printing to hardware hacking with arduinos and more.

Provides an opportunity for youth between the ages of 6 and 13 to work collaboratively in a fun and innovative learning environment.

As Girls Learning Code grew in popularity, we began receiving inquiries from parents looking for learning opportunities for all children. In response to this community need, Kids Learning Code was created. Like Girls Learning Code, these programs are beginner-friendly, hands-on and fun. Our 4:1 ratio of kids to mentors is maintained with balanced numbers of male and female mentors.

Offers teens an introduction to code while also teaching valuable collaborative skills and providing opportunities for mentorship.

Our educational program for youth between the ages of 13 and 17 years old is about much more than learning to code! The goal of this program is to help young people build the technical skills, soft skills, confidence, and communities that will propel them forward in their future personal and professional endeavours. High school students are encouraged to join the Teen Club to be invited to attend technical workshops, industry field trips and volunteer opportunities all year round that will help them to envision and prepare for a future in technology.

Demystifies coding for non-technical educators and empowers them to implement technical learning in their classrooms.

Teachers Learning Code is our newest initiative, introduced in the Summer of 2016. This program aims to empower traditional and nontraditional educators to introduce coding into their classrooms and communities. Program content has been inspired by our experiences teaching thousands of youth across Canada to code, and informed by the insights gathered through this work. Together with teachers and non-traditional educators, we will inspire the next generation of Canadian technologists.
